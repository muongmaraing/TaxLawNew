## 1. YouTube Community Post Formatting Reference

YouTube Community Posts support a limited subset of Markdown-like formatting. Here's the complete reference:

| Formatting | Syntax | Example | Notes |
|------------|--------|---------|-------|
| **Bold** | `**text**` | **Tax Law** | Most impactful — use for headers & key terms |
| *Italic* | `*text*` | *Deadline extended* | Subtle emphasis, less visible on mobile |
| ~~Strikethrough~~ | `-text-` | ~~Old rate: 10%~~ | Use for outdated info or "before/after" |
| Bullet list | `- item` | - File by March 31 | MUST have space after dash |
| Numbered list | `1. item` | 1. Register | MUST have period after number |
| Line break | `[empty line]` | (creates paragraph break) | Single enter = soft break, double = paragraph |
| Hashtag | `#topic` | #CambodiaTax | 1-3 max recommended |
| Emoji | 🎯📌✅❌📊 | (paste or type code) | Use strategically, not excessively |

**What YouTube does NOT support:**
- Headers (`# Heading`, `## Heading`) — NOT rendered, shows as plain text
- Links (clickable hyperlinks) — shows as plain text
- Blockquotes (`> text`) — shows as plain text  
- Code blocks or inline code
- Tables
- Horizontal rules (`---`)
- Images in text body
- @mentions (no user tagging)
- Colored text

### ⚠️ Critical Limitation: Post Truncation
YouTube truncates Community Posts after approximately 275-350 characters in the feed. Users must click **"…more"** to see the full post. This means:

1. **The first 2-3 lines (the preview) are CRITICAL** — they determine if someone clicks "more"
2. **Lead with the hook** — put your most engaging content first
3. **Don't bury the CTA** — if you want people to watch a video, mention it in the first ~280 chars
4. **Long posts still work** — but only ~30% of viewers expand them

## 2. Post Structures That Drive Engagement (Educational Channels)

Based on analysis of top finance/education channels (Graham Stephan, Andrei Jikh, The Plain Bagel, AccountingStuff, BiggerPockets, and Asian education channels):

### Structure #1: The "Mini-Lesson" (BEST for small channels)
A self-contained educational snippet that provides value immediately — no expansion required.

```
[EMOJI] [BOLD TOPIC HEADER]

[1-2 sentences of key insight or tip]

• [Key point 1]
• [Key point 2]  
• [Key point 3]

[EMOJI] [Question to audience]

#[hashtag1] #[hashtag2]
```

**Why it works:** Even without clicking "more," the reader learns something. This builds authority and trust.

### Structure #2: The "Sneak Peek" (for new video promotion)
Teases the video content and creates curiosity.

```
🚨 New video just dropped!

**[Video Title Hook]**

Here's what I cover:
👆 [Point 1]
👆 [Point 2]
👆 [Point 3]

[EMOJI] Go watch now: [link in description]

What do you think about [question]?
```

### Structure #3: The "Q&A / Poll" (highest comment engagement)
Invites the audience to participate and share their experience.

```
**[QUESTION HEADER]**

[Context setting — 1 sentence]

A) [Option 1]
B) [Option 2]
C) [Option 3]
D) [Something unexpected]

👇 Drop your answer in the comments!
```

### Structure #4: The "Checklist / Reminder" (high save/screenshot rate)
Lists action items — useful for tax deadlines and compliance topics.

```
📌 **[TOPIC] CHECKLIST**

✅ [ ] Action item 1
✅ [ ] Action item 2
✅ [ ] Action item 3
✅ [ ] Action item 4

📥 Save this for later!

#TaxTip #[Topic]
```

### Structure #5: The "Myth Buster" (high shareability)
Debunks a common misconception — drives comments and shares.

```
❌ **MYTH:** [Common misconception]

✅ **TRUTH:** [Correct information]

[Brief explanation — 1-2 sentences]

💬 Were you taught this myth too?
```

## 4. Best Practices for Specific Elements

### Post Length
| Context | Optimal Length | Max Before Truncation |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| Mini-lesson (no image) | 150-250 chars | 275 chars (keep hook visible) |
| Full educational post | 400-600 chars | Can go longer, but lead with hook |
| Video promo | 200-350 chars | Put video title in first 100 chars |
| Poll/question | 100-200 chars | Keep it punchy |

### Emoji Placement Strategy
- **Opening emoji**: Use 1 emoji at the very start — grabs attention in the feed
- **Section divider**: Use 1 emoji per section to visually break content
- **Bullet replacement**: Use emojis like ✅, 👆, 📌, ⏰ instead of `-` for visual variety
- **Closing emoji**: Ends with an emoji to feel complete and approachable

**Recommended emojis for tax/finance content:**
| Category | Recommended Emojis |
|----------|-------------------|
| Money/Tax | 💰 📊 📈 📉 🏦 💳 🧾 📋 |
| Alerts/Deadlines | ⏰ 📌 🚨 ⚠️ 🔔 📅 🗓️ |
| Actions | ✅ ❌ ➡️ 👆 📥 📤 |
| Education | 📚 🎓 💡 🧠 🔍 📝 |
| Cambodia/Khmer | 🇰🇭 |
| Celebration | 🎉 🏆 ⭐ 🔥 👏 |
| Questions | ❓ 💬 🤔 🗳️ |
| Lists | 📋 ✅ 🔢 • |

### Call-to-Action (CTA) Best Practices
1. **Place CTA in the preview zone** (first 275 chars) for maximum visibility
2. **Be specific** — instead of "Let me know," say "Drop your tax bracket in the comments"
3. **Use emoji + question combo** — `💬 What's your biggest tax headache?`
4. **Video CTAs**: "👆 Full breakdown in new video [link below]" works better than "watch my video"
5. **Save CTAs**: Use `📥 Save this for tax season!` for checklist posts
6. **Subscriber CTAs**: `🔔 Turn on notifications so you don't miss deadlines!` (subtle, value-focused)

### Hashtag Strategy
- **1-3 hashtags maximum** — more looks spammy and reduces engagement
- **Mix of reach + specificity**:
  - Broad: `#TaxTip` `#Accounting` `#Cambodia`
  - Niche: `#CambodiaTax` `#PPTax` `#SMEAccounting`
- **Put hashtags on their own line** at the very bottom of the post
- **Don't hashtag in the middle** of sentences
- **Branded hashtag**: Create `#MaraingTaxTips` for your channel

## 7. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### ❌ Pitfall 1: Posting too long without a hook
**Problem:** 500+ char post with no compelling first sentence → no one clicks "more"  
**Solution:** Lead with bold text or a question that demands an answer

### ❌ Pitfall 2: Too many hashtags
**Problem:** `#tax #cambodia #accounting #smallbusiness #finance #money #SME`  
**Solution:** Max 3 hashtags. `#CambodiaTax #TaxTip #SME`

### ❌ Pitfall 3: Posting only video promos
**Problem:** Every post says "Watch my new video" → feels spammy, low engagement  
**Solution:** 70% value posts, 30% video promos

### ❌ Pitfall 4: No emojis or all-emoji
**Problem:** Wall of text = skippable. All emoji = unprofessional  
**Solution:** 3-5 emojis in a post, used as visual anchors

### ❌ Pitfall 5: Inconsistent posting
**Problem:** Posting 5 times one week, then nothing for 2 weeks  
**Solution:** Stick to a schedule (even 2x/week) — consistency builds the habit in your audience

### ❌ Pitfall 6: Not replying to comments
**Problem:** Someone comments and you never respond → they won't comment again  
**Solution:** Reply to every comment within 24 hours — this is how you build a community from 38 subscribers

### ❌ Pitfall 7: Posting at wrong times
**Problem:** Posting at midnight Cambodia time when your audience is asleep  
**Solution:** Post during business hours (7-8 AM, 12-1 PM, 6-8 PM)

## 9. Growth Strategy: From 38 to 100+ Subscribers

At 38 subscribers, **every comment is gold**. Strategy for Community Posts at this stage:

1. **Post consistency** (3x/week minimum) — be present
2. **Ask questions** that invite responses — reply to every single one
3. **Post value that doesn't require clicking** — give away free knowledge
4. **Cross-promote subtly** — "I covered this in detail in my latest video" not "GO WATCH NOW"
5. **Build the "Maraing" brand** — use the same emoji set, tone, and format every time
